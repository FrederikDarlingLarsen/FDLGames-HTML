Ancient Storytelling: Increasing Reading Engagement

This was the semester project on my sixth and final semester of my bachelors degree at medialogy. The idea behind this project was to create an interactive version of an ancient story used in Danish High Schools for the "fag oldtidskundsab". This would then be used instead of the regular text as homework to prepare the students for their next class. The larger purpose for the project was to increase reading engagement among high school students in Denmark, since danish youth are seriously lacking behind other OECD countries in reading capabilites.

The story presented in this interactive experience, is the first story from Herodot, which is about the King Kandaules and his Queen. The story is about how Kandaules' guard Gyges is forced by Kandaules to get look at his wife naked, in order to afirm how beutiful she is. The queen "finds this out" and then presents Gyges with the dielmma of either murdering the king to become the new king and marry the queen or otherwise to be killed immediately. He chooses to be king and the oracle of delphi decides that he indeed is the true king but that in "femte led" he vill be avenged.

The experince was created with the Unity Engine. The player has to find different items pertaining to the sory in order to unlock different freeze frame scenarios that tell parts of the story. The freeze frames consist of human ghost-like characters in differnt poses that represent the characters in the story along with various props. A voice over is also implemented to supbelment the text that is presented in the freeze frames. The voice over system was reused from our previous project "Chiseling The Story".

ECO-Frog

This game was developed on the Game Design course i took on the sixth semester of medialogy. It was made by a group consisting of me and two other students. Most of the code was written by me and I also designed the levels. One group member worked of the frog model, anmations and controls, while another worked on UI and sound.

Since the theme on the course was to create either an ECO game or a game that would somehow promote the 17 UN sustainabillty goals, we decided to create a game about a frog the would collect trash while on the journey to deliver a message to humans about the sad state of his forest. An intersting mechanic implemented for this game was the controls. Instead of using tranitaional WASD controls the player would instead have to click on the sceen in the direction they wanted the frog to jump and hold the mouse button the charge the jump.

The game was creted in the Unity engine, and working on this project was another change to practice working with this, which i at this point had gotten very familiar with. The final game was very much based on some early "quick and dirty" prototypes that i put together in a couple of days, so I found that working with the Unity Engine had become quite easy, compared to when a started worknig with it some years ago.

Turbo Cow (2018)

I attended a game jam at Vallekilde Højskole() in Holbæk in 2018 with some friends. We did not reasise unitl we got there that almost all attendants were professional game developers, which meant that we got to see the working progress of differnt indie game while we worked on our own game. This jam lasted a bit longer than the usual 48 hours, which means that we go to spend more time making a game that was much better than what we had done on other jams.

The game is a racing game, with procurally generated chunks of race track, where the player has to keep racing, endless runner style in order to best the high score. The idea was to create a very stylixed game utilixing the at the time relatively new HDRP(High Definition Render Pipeline) in Unity. We settled on a synthwave style game, with a soundtrack to match. One intersting feture that was added was to be able to play the game in synth maode or 8 bit mode. The would change the stlye of the music and a filter would be added to the screen in 8-bit to make it look like and old CRT monitor.

For this game I worked primarily on the sound fx an the muisc. I got to play around with LMMS for the music. We had some conversations about releasing the game on Steam, and chipped in a Steam direct page for the release. We never got to finish the game though, due to the three of us being busy with life stuff.
